Blue Hydrogen

Hydrogen will play a significant role in future energy supply. In addition to production of renewable natural gas (RNG), G4 technology can also be used in the production of Blue Hydrogen (H2 from fossil natural gas with carbon capture and storage).

Although G4 Blue Hydrogen is not renewable, it leverages available biomass to produce very large quantities of negative carbon intensity hydrogen and plays a critical role in the transition to a green hydrogen future.

G4 RNG used to produce renewable hydrogen

G4 Blue Hydrogen is produced by using fossil natural gas as the SMR feed stock and G4 RNG as the fuel for SMR burner. The RNG production also produces biochar to sequester carbon.

Removal of CO2 from Flue Gas

The combustion of RNG in the SMR burner results in a carbon neutral flue gas which eliminates the requirement for expensive and complex carbon capture technology to remove CO2 from flue gas.

Purifying the SMR reformate

The SMR reformate is purified by a G4 gas separation system which partitions the reformate into a pure hydrogen stream and a pure CO2 stream. The combination of biochar production and CO2 sequestration provides a negative carbon intensity value for G4 Blue Hydrogen.
